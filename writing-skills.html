Upskill: Improve your writing skills. Become more employable locally and internationally with the help of the Global Writing Centre (GWC). Register for our Workplace Writing Course (WWC) and get REAL value for your money!!


Writing skills are among key communication skills many workplaces expect their employees to have, especially in today’s globalised economies that are driven by data, information, and knowledge. Many employers, both locally and globally, are looking for employees who can express themselves clearly and effectively, using different kinds of written workplace genres: memos, reports, submissions, minutes, articles, etc.

As part of our commitment to upskill the Namibian workforce for the 21st century workplace, Global Links & Consultants cc is offering you, among other things, opportunities to improve your writing skills, which are required in the knowledge-driven economy.

Because the global market has shifted towards valuing skills more than qualifications, emphasis on our courses is placed on the acquisition of know-how by doing. Unlike many writing courses offered in the country, we focus on the processes that writers go through and the products they produce. Moreover, our writing courses consider the social aspects of written communications and not just the cognitive ones.

Course Learning Outcomes

By the end of the course, participants should be able to:

  • write memos, letters, minutes, submissions, reports, and articles
  • identify purpose and analyse audiences
  • use correct register
  • assess texts
  • write in drafts
  • improve language
  • edit texts

Module 1: Memos, letters, and reports

  • Analyse memos, letters, reports, and articles
  • Analyse purpose and audience
  • Examine the writing process

Module 2: Minutes

    Practice minute-taking; analyse different types of minutes, identify the purpose(s) of minutes and structure minutes

Module 3: Reports

    Analyse different types of reports; identify purposes and audiences; structuring reports; the writing process; using graphics; practice writing reports; editing and presentation.

    NB: All modules include language work based on the learners’ main needs. Also, there the course is designed to foster self-directed learning.

Assessment

    Learners are assessed through individual assignments, class exercises, class-based group projects, oral presentations and writing portfolio.

Duration

    The duration of our short courses is five days.

    Cost: N$2500 per person (for training and learning materials only)

    NB: We provide after-training support for 1 month to consolidate the skills acquired during training and ensure transfer of training to the workplace.
